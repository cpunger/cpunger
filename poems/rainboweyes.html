Rainbow Eyes

Half sunk, rainbow eyes tend to the ocean. Dive gradually deeper. Where pressure presses down. Discover fish never found. Tend to curiosity through muffled ears and goggle-glass vision. Find that there’s something here. Really here. A statue. A chest. A river. A tongue. A womb. A penis. A lung. a lung. a lung. Water-filled ears. And, gills gills gills. A blob of pinkish-orangish-red. And— sand, seaweed, a breast, a bite? Two pennies? What’s found there? Float up— meter by meter, so lungs don’t collapse. Take in oxygen-tank gas. At the edge where water meets air, swim to stony shore. Climb upon it. What’s under there? It’s for him to know. . . ’less you dare to dare. I never dare.
