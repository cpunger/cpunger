Another Month

I saw a man standing next to a sign holding pamphlets— something about God, and savior, and damnation. He wore a trench coat, a fedora, and sunglasses— like he was undercover. I decided to approach. He asked: “What do you want?” I told him my wish: “Another year.” And he said: “I’ll give it to you, but each month— I need something in return. In January, I need every hair, plucked fresh from the root— so they are good for use.” So, I spent the month of January plucking my hairs one by one. I saved them up in a large plastic bag— clear— and froze them. To my delight, the man approved. I lived another month. In February and March, he asked for my skin. Each layer peeled— isolated— so they were good for use. So I did— the epidermis, and each coat of it first. Stratum: corneum, luciderm, granulosum, spinosum, and basale. After the epidermis, the dermis and the hypodermis. I peeled until I was: muscle bone organs veins nerves eyes lungs and I lived another month.
