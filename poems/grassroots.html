Grass Roots

I’ve got somewhere stupid to be. So I have to go, excuse me. To farewell— I’ve lost friends, possibly possible lovers, a cash gift returned. But I do it again. What’s the difference between leaving and staying? What’s the difference between losing and being blue? What’s between the lawn and the dirt? I don’t really fucking know much about it. But—there are roly-polys and worms. And grass roots. (If they’re the golf-course-steroid-sanitized kind that soak up water like some perpetually dried sponge just to be greener than the rest— and flaunt.) Bugs still crawl and creep, but— it ain’t the same as when green ain’t lime and don’t distract me from cloudless sky: blue. No rain. No chance. For years. (Four years?) Forget me like I hope to forget HGH grass. Don’t gift me goodbyes. Don’t send me letters. Don’t expel an utter. But— I’ll take a farewell kiss, ‘cause, I’m going somewhere stupid again.
