You Are Tall, Little One

You are tall. Me? Yes, you. No, I am not. No? No, I am not. I am not tall at all. I am very very small. How so? Can’t you tell? Can’t you see? It’s in my eyes. Look! Look closely! I am smaller than the twinkle in a roach’s eye. I am smaller than a speck of dust, on the back of a fly. I could float through air and never catch a strand of light I could drift between brick— not colliding with a particle— yet, inaudible, shyly say, “Sorry! Please! Excuse me!” But . . . Though you say You are so small. I see you. And . . . You are dear to me. Dearer than you know. A million deer would have no chance at overwhelming your dearness to me, little one.
