you are tall

You are tall.

Me?

Yes, you.

No, I am not.

No?

No, I am not. I am not tall at all. I am very very small.

How so?

Can’t you tell? Can’t you see? It’s in my eyes. Look! Look closely...

I am smaller than the twinkle in a roach’s eye.

I am smaller than a speck of dust, on the back of a fly.

I could float through air and never catch a strand of light.

I could drift between brick— not collide with a particle—

yet, inaudible, shyly say, “Sorry! Please! Excuse me!”

But...

Though you say you are so small.

I see you.

And you are dear to me. Dearer than you know.

A million deer would have no chance at overwhelming your dearness to me, little one.
