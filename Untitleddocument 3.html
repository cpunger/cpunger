      <h2>you are tall</h2>

      <p>

        You are tall<br>

        You are tall.<br>

        Me?<br>

        Yes, you.<br>

        No, I am not.<br>

        No?<br>

        No, I am not.<br>

        I am not tall at all.<br>

        I am very very small.<br>

        How so?<br>

        Can’t you tell?<br>

        Can’t you see?<br>

        It’s in my eyes. Look! Look closely.<br>

        I am smaller than the twinkle<br>

        in a roach’s eye.<br>

        I am smaller than a speck of dust,<br>

        on the back of a fly.<br>

        I could float through air<br>

        and never catch<br>

        a strand of light<br>

        I could drift between brick—<br>

        not colliding with a particle—<br>

        yet, inaudible, shyly say,<br>

        “Sorry! Please! Excuse me!”<br><br>

        But,<br>

        Though you say<br>

        You are so small.<br>

        I see you.<br>

        And,<br>

        You are dear to me.<br>

        Dearer than you know.<br>

        A million deer would have no chance<br>

        At overwhelming<br>

        Your dearness to me,<br>

        Little one.

      </p>

    <div class="poem">

      <h2>scabs</h2>

      <p>

        The little shrimp<br>

        set out<br>

        on a dare<br>

        to find its fate<br>

        in seaweed hair.<br>

        She swam<br>

        down<br>

        and down<br>

        and down<br>

        until she was…<br>

        lost as fuck.<br>

        A whale swam and<br>

        sucked—<br>

        and in went little shrimp<br>

        through elongated tusks,<br>

        into a dark cave mouth,<br>

        its tongue rough and brown.<br>

        Upon reeking muscle<br>

        sat a fish,<br>

        sorting through<br>

        file cabinets,<br>

        each drawer spilling:<br>

        one held glasses,<br>

        another—bowls,<br>

        another—forks.<br>

        The bottom drawer—<br>

        something dark red,<br>

        dry and crusty:<br>

        scabs.<br>

        The fish that filed<br>

        seemed familiar,<br>

        but the shrimp<br>

        couldn’t place her.<br>

        Filing fish swam around,<br>

        side-eyeing the child.<br>

        Seeing her made her…<br>

        want to go back to filing scabs.<br>

        But the shrimp<br>

        couldn’t contain<br>

        the need to ask:<br>

        “Are you okay?”<br>

        To which the fish<br>

        flared her gills,<br>

        filtered salt solution,<br>

        and said,<br>

        “Not really.”<br><br>

        Little shrimp<br>

        pried,<br>

        “What’s wrong?”<br>

        Filing fish let out a snarl—<br>

        “Beat it, kid. Enjoy yourself.<br>

        Life’s a little bit<br>

        of this and that—<br>

        scabs and bowls<br>

        that you never know<br>

        exactly what to do with.”

      </p>
