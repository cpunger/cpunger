      <b>You are tall.<br></b>

        <p>

        <i>Me?<br></i>

        <p>

        <b>Yes, you.<br></b>

        <p>

        <i>No, I am not.<br></i>

        <p>

        <b>No?<br></b>

        <p>

        <i>No, I am not.<br>

        I am not tall at all.<br>

        I am very very small.<br></i>

        <p>

        <b>How so?<br></b>

        <p>

        <i>Can’t you tell?<br>

        Can’t you see?<br>

        It’s in my eyes. Look! Look closely...<br>

        <p>

        I am smaller than the twinkle<br>

        in a roach’s eye.<br>

        <p>

        I am smaller than a speck of dust,<br>

        on the back of a fly.<br>

        <p>

        I could float through air<br>

        and never catch<br>

        a strand of light<br>

        <p>

        I could drift between brick—<br>

        not collide with a particle—<br>

        <p>

        yet, inaudible, shyly say,<br>

        <p>

        “Sorry! Please! Excuse me!”<br><br></i>

        <p>

        <p>

        <b>But...<br>

        <p>

        though you say<br>

        you are so small.<br>

        <p>

        <i>I see you.<br></i>

        <p>

        And <i>you</i> are <i>dear</i> to me.<br>

        <p>

        <i>Dearer</i> than you know.<br>

        <p>

        A <i>million deer</i> would have <i>no chance</i><br>

        at overwhelming<br>

        <p>

        <i>your dearness to me,</i><br>
