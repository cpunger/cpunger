Runner Duck

Runner duck, is runner-up for second to last. you have no wings— but there you go, waddling, as fast as your webbed toes can Runner duck— you wonder, "why did they name me that, if I'm not fast?" But, runner duck, it’s not the time that counts— it’s the people at the line that count, ticking themsleves. It doesn’t mean you’re good at it— or bad at it. Someone’s got to be second to last. Everyone’s got some “place” to be- since they order things that way. But— you're so cute and earnest— real earnest. I like that about you. They didn’t hold a contest for those things, did they? Runner duck, don’t mind them. Quack, waddle, honk your tune. Because you are a runner— duck, no matter where they place you. And you are runner-up, or first, to me— if I believed in such a thing, runner duck.
