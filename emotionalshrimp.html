scabs

The little shrimp set out on a dare to find its fate in seaweed hair. She swam down and down and down . . . until she was . . . lost as fuck. A whale swam and sucked— and in went little shrimp through elongated tusks into dark cave mouth- its tongue rough and brown. Upon reeking muscle sat a fish sorting through file cabinets- each drawer spilling. One held glasses. Another- bowls. Another- forks. The bottom drawer— something dark red dry and crusty: scabs. The fish that filed seemed familiar, but the shrimp couldn’t place her. Filing fish swam around, side-eyeing the child. Seeing her made her. . . Want to go back to filing scabs. . . But the shrimp couldn’t contain the need to ask: "Are you okay?” To which the fish flared her gills, filtered salt solution, and said: “Not really.” Little shrimp pried: “What’s wrong??” Filing fish let out a snarl— “Beat it, kid. Enjoy yourself. Life’s a little bit of this and that— scabs and bowls you never really know exactly what to do with.”
