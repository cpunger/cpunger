scabs

The little shrimp set out on a dare to find its fate in seaweed hair. She swam down and down and down until she was… lost as fuck. A whale swam and sucked— and in went little shrimp through elongated tusks, into a dark cave mouth, its tongue rough and brown. Upon reeking muscle sat a fish, sorting through file cabinets, each drawer spilling: one held glasses, another—bowls, another—forks. The bottom drawer— something dark red, dry and crusty: scabs. The fish that filed seemed familiar, but the shrimp couldn’t place her. Filing fish swam around, side-eyeing the child. Seeing her made her… want to go back to filing scabs. But the shrimp couldn’t contain the need to ask: “Are you okay?” To which the fish flared her gills, filtered salt solution, and said, “Not really.” Little shrimp pried, “What’s wrong?” Filing fish let out a snarl— “Beat it, kid. Enjoy yourself. Life’s a little bit of this and that— scabs and bowls that you never know exactly what to do with.”
